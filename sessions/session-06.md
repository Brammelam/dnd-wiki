## Recap

The party arrived in **[Dior](/places/dior)** after a long journey of four to five days. The city lay nestled in mountains, accessed through a steep road leading into a large tunnel flanked by two impressive statues. Emerging on the other side revealed a circular valley with an artificial lake at its center. A great temple rose from the middle of the lake, and the city was divided into a morning side and an evening side.

They chose **[Mountain Dew's Rest](/places/mountain-dews-rest)** as their base — a modest inn run by a dwarf bartender. The innkeeper told them about the academy on the morning side and the city's strict building codes preventing any structure from exceeding the mountain height.

### The Academy

The next morning they visited **[the Academy](/places/the-academy)**, which looked more like a library than a school. **[Gilda](/characters/gilda)**, an elderly dwarf woman with curly hair and vision problems, worked the reception desk. [Fenris](/characters/fenris) offered her a pair of glasses from [Alverike](/places/alverike), but they didn't suit her vision. After hard negotiation, Gilda bought them for **300 gold** (having initially tried 200).

They arranged to meet **[Toltec](/characters/toltec)** — the Dragonborn academic they needed to deliver a mysterious box to — the following evening at seven. Asking around, they learned Toltec was a dark-green-scaled Dragonborn with an owl-like appearance, one of three Dragonborn in the city.

### Shopping

At **[Bob Swords](/characters/bob-swords)**' shop, the jovial hobbit showed them magical armor including a frost leather vest, a standard vest, and expensive runed armor. He confirmed that magical armor would adapt to a shapeshifter's form. Bob referred them to **[Tore Idle](/characters/tore-idle)**, a Dragonborn smith who repaired [Voris](/characters/voris)'s damaged bow for 50 gold, offering an upgraded version with more power at the cost of aiming difficulty.

Bob also mentioned a friend named **[Carlos](/characters/carlos)** on the shadow side who dealt in less legitimate business.

### The Crypt Deal

On the shadow side, they found Carlos in a dim bar reminiscent of a cantina. [Peren](/characters/peren) greeted him in Spanish, earning immediate rapport. Carlos revealed he had found an **alternative entrance** to the Klypten — a 700-year-old sealed crypt no one had entered in centuries. He offered: they go in, get treasure, he takes 25% plus fencing commission. His bad knee prevented him from entering himself.

They agreed to meet at ten that evening. Carlos led them to the entrance — through the back of a cellar near the temple, where workers were preparing for a wedding. Behind loose planks lay a natural crack in the rock.

### Entering the Crypt

Inside, they found ancient dwarven runes, statues of dwarves holding helmets, and thick dust everywhere. They came to a warning sign about holy ground and skeletal remains. They chose the left door, passing a stairway to a gallery. A suspicious step was carefully avoided.

They explored the gallery, found a hole in the wall as a possible escape route, then descended to a stone door flanked by **four dwarf statues** holding different weapons (hammer, axe, sword, morningstar). Each had four buttons. After working out the code — pressing buttons in sequence — the door opened. [Gal'Rok](/characters/galrok) went first and triggered a **magical trap**: luminous threads tried to pull him apart, dislocating his shoulder before the others cut him free.

The session ended with the party pushing deeper into the crypt.
